The partitioned-domain multiscale modeling methods divide the computational domain to two subdomains: atomistic and continuum...

Therefore, the partitioned-domain methods works in a way that the atomistic subdomain is applied for defects or large deformation gradient while the continuum subdomain is applied where the deformation gradient is small ...

There are various current and potential applications for multiscale analysis. Concurrent and Hierarchical Multiscale techniques are useful for different applications.

Concurrent Multiscale Applications

  • To provide realistic boundary conditions to detailed nano scale phenomena.
  • For developing atomistic-based cohesive zone models for meso-macro scale crack propagation.
  • Investigation of Dislocation nucleation mechanisms
  • Origins of plasticity mechanisms.
  • Nano-indentation and scratch test modeling.

Hierarchical Multiscale Applications

  • Modeling atomistically derived bulk behavior at meso and macro scales.
  • Meso-scale dislocation dynamics in large volumes.
  • Material response to high speed impaction.
  • Polycrystalline material behavior including the grain boundary phenomena.
For individual multiscale methods and their applications, please refer to our multiscale methods page.
